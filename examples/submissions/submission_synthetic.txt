The most dangerous villain is not always the one who kills the most people. Sometimes the worst villain is the person who turns ordinary ambition into a way of controlling everyone around him.

Daniel Plainview is deeply immoral because he treats other people as tools. He claims to care about family and business, but his actions show that he mostly cares about winning. His relationship with H.W. is complicated because there are moments that seem affectionate, yet Daniel also uses him as part of his public image.

Anton Chigurh is terrifying because he kills without ordinary emotion. His coin toss makes murder seem like fate, but it is really a way for him to avoid responsibility for his own choices.

Lou Bloom is also evil because he learns how to profit from suffering. Unlike Chigurh, he does not need a weapon to harm people. He uses cameras, language, and business ambition to turn violence into opportunity.

Overall, Daniel Plainview may be the most morally corrupt because his evil grows out of values that society often rewards: ambition, competition, wealth, and domination.
